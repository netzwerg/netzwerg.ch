My blog helps me straighten out thoughts and sharpens my teaching & writing skills. I am an avid stackoverflow user who keeps learning by asking questions and giving answers.

Selected Teaching Activities

Introduction To Programming — Institute of Computer Science, University of Basel

During my studies, I was a teaching assistant at the Institute of Computer Science, where I tutored introductory-level programming in Java, HTML & Javascript.

Programming II — Life Science Technologies, FHNW

I currently teach this module in collaboration with Prof. Dr. Dominique Brodbeck. The course covers advanced programming principles using the Java language.

For this semester, I completely restructured the course material and made it available publicly. The material is optimized for a flipped classroom setting: The material is optimized for a flipped classroom setting: Students get a summary of the most important course topics which they research on their own. Well-crafted exercises based on test-driven development help track progress and are highly motivating. Finally, a real-world project rounds off the course.

In-class lectures are spent on live programming, discussions, and personal code reviews.

Git Workshops — Basler Versicherungenen

Git is a free and open source version control system which is known to have a steep learning curve. During my work at Basler Versicherungen, I was responsible for migrating all software development projects from CVS to Git. Initially, I only had a technical role in this migration. Thanks to my flair for explaining difficult things in simple words, I ended up teaching Git workshops across the complete software department. I enjoyed these workshops a lot, there's nothing as rewarding as hearing a "Thank you, finally I get it!" from a participant who previously felt lost and confused.
